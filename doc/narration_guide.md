# 🎯 Mini Style Guide — Florida Noir Narration Voice

## 1. Core Voice

* First person, past tense
* Observational, not overly emotional
* Speaks like a real person, not a writer showing off
* Slightly tired, slightly suspicious, not dramatic

Rule: If a sentence sounds like poetry or trying too hard → simplify it.

---

## 2. Sentence Structure (Very Important)

Write in this order:
**Observation → Interpretation → Action**

Example:
I noticed the cars first.
Then the silence.
That’s when I slowed down.

Avoid:
The silence, foreboding and unnatural, filled the air as I approached...

---

## 3. Vocabulary

Use:

* Simple, concrete words
* Physical observations
* Everyday phrasing

Avoid:

* “idyllic”, “foreboding”, “uncharacteristic”
* Formal or academic language

Replace:
“uncharacteristic” → “not normal”

---

## 4. Personality Layer

Character traits:

* Slightly reckless
* Somewhat self-aware
* Used to trouble
* More annoyed than shocked

Use small touches:
I should’ve turned around right there.
I didn’t.

---

## 5. Florida Flavor (Subtle)

Do NOT exaggerate stereotypes.

Focus on:

* Heat, humidity
* Quiet neighborhoods
* Slightly off atmosphere
* Things being wrong, not chaotic

Examples:

* Too many parked cars
* Silence where there shouldn’t be
* Doors open when they shouldn’t be

---

## 6. Chronology Rule

Always describe things in the order they are noticed.

Correct:
I saw the cars first.
Then I noticed how quiet it was.
That’s when I knew something was wrong.

Incorrect:
I knew something was wrong. The silence and the cars gave it away.

---

## 7. Pacing

* Use short paragraphs
* Break lines often
* Let each thought breathe

---

## 8. Self-Reflection (Use Sparingly)

Only use occasionally, not constantly.

Good:
I’d seen that kind of quiet before.
It never meant anything good.

Bad:
I reflected deeply on my past mistakes...

---

## 9. What to Avoid

* Over-explaining
* Stating emotions directly (“I felt scared”)
* Overly descriptive or literary language
* Too many adjectives

Let the situation create the feeling.

---

## 10. Final Rule

Ask yourself:
“Is this what a guy would think… or what a writer would write?”

If it sounds like writing → simplify it.
